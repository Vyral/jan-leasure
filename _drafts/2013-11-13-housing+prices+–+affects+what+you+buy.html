Housing Prices – Affects What You Buy

You have heard by now that now is the time to act. Now is the time to buy. But why?

Housing prices have gone up by 5 percent in the last year and interest rates have increased by about a point. That may not seem like a lot, but let’s look at how the increase affects your purchasing power.

A year ago you could buy a $180,000, put a down a payment of 10 percent and have a principal and interest payment of $725 a month.

This year that same home is now $189,000. With the increase of interest rates added, your monthly payment is now $862. That’s more than one hundred dollars in difference each month.

If your budget is $725, you must scale back the price range of homes you’re looking at to around $160,000.
